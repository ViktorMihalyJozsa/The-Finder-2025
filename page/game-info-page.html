Using the Finder Game

In the Finder game, the goal is to avoid all hidden targets on the game board and reveal all other fields.

Click the left mouse button on the fields to reveal them; the number on the revealed field shows how many targets are in the 8 neighboring fields that you need to avoid.

If you are sure that a field has a target underneath, mark it with a flag using the right mouse button (this prevents accidental revealing).

If a field hides a target and you click on it, you lose the game.


Basic Gameplay

• Left Mouse Button: Click on a field to reveal it.

• Right Mouse Button: Flag the suspected target, or another right click removes the flag.

• Reveal: If you discover a field marked with a number, that number will show the number of adjacent targets.

• Auto Reveal: If all adjacent fields around a numbered field have flags, and the number of adjacent fields is the same as the number, the machine will automatically reveal the remaining unmarked adjacent fields.


Tips and Strategies

• Start in the middle: It is often a good idea to start revealing towards the middle of the board, as this will free up more space in one move.

• Watch the numbers: If you have marked as many targets around a numbered field as If the number is the same, all other adjacent spaces are free of targets and you can safely reveal them as well.

• Don't over-mark: Only flag a space if you are sure. Marking flags is not necessarily the fastest way to win.

• Use patterns: Look for patterns, such as the 1-2-1 pattern, which indicates that there is definitely a target under the center square.


Use keyboard shortcuts (if available):

• F2: Start a new game.

• Enter: Reveal a square (when the mouse is over the square).

• Space: Toggle flag/question mark (when the mouse is over the square).


Be patient:

If you are not sure about your next move, slow down and think about your options.

The most important thing is logical deduction, not speed.
